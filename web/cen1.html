Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY)

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) is a major initiative launched by the Government of India in 2015 to improve irrigation facilities and ensure "Har Khet Ko Pani" (Water to Every Field). The aim of this scheme is to enhance water use efficiency, promote per drop more crop, and ensure that more agricultural land gets access to irrigation for better productivity.

Key Features of Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY):
  • Expand irrigation coverage to more farmland.
  • Improve water use efficiency in agriculture.
  • Promote micro-irrigation systems like drip and sprinkler.
  • Encourage water conservation and rainwater harvesting.
  • Ensure integrated development of water sources, including ground and surface water.
  • Reduce farmers' dependency on monsoon by providing sustainable water resources.

Benefits:
  • Increases crop productivity through assured irrigation.
  • Reduces dependency on rainfall and protects farmers from drought risk.
  • Promotes efficient water use and reduces wastage.
  • Improves income and livelihood of farmers.
  • Enhances groundwater recharge and long-term sustainability of agriculture.
